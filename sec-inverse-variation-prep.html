    Section 16.1 Inverse Variation and Negative Powers Prep Activities

    Two varying quantities \(Q_1\) and \(Q_2\) have an inversely proportional relationship when the product of the quantities is constant. That is, \(Q_1\) and \(Q_2\) have an inversely proportional relationship when
    \begin{equation*} c = Q_2 Q_1 \end{equation*}
    always has the same value. We call \(c\) the constant of proportionality.
    🔗

    Prep Activity 16.1.3.

    Suppose that the number of hours, \(h\text{,}\) required to build a fence is inversely proportional to the number of people, \(n\text{,}\) working on the fence.
    🔗

    (a)

    If it takes \(4\) people \(31\) hours to build the fence, what is \(c\text{,}\) the constant of proportionality?
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Use the definition of inverse proportionality and your constant of proportionality to write an equation that shows the relationship between \(h\) and \(n\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Use your equation to predict how long it will take \(11\) people to build the fence.
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Use your equation to predict how many people need to be on the team in order to build the fence in 8 hours.
