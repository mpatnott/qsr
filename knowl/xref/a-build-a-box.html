Activity 5.3.3.

Pablo and Aisha are designing custom boxes to use at a special event. Attendees will fill their boxes with candy from a candy bar They’ll make the boxes out of \(8.5 \times 11\) inch cardstock that they’ve had printed with their club’s logo and advertisements. In this activity, you’ll help Pablo and Aisha figure out how to build a box that will hold the most candy possible.
The diagram below shows how Pablo and Aisha are going to make their boxes. They’ll cut out the corner squares, then fold up the four edge rectangles. The center rectangle will be the bottom of the box.

(a)

Explain why \(x\) is the height of the box.

(b)

Find formulas for the length and width of the box.

(c)

Find a formula for the volume of the box.

(d)

Use your formula to find the volume of the box for three different values of \(x\text{.}\) (You choose the values of \(x\)).

(e)

Use Desmos to make graph your volume formula, and sketch your graph here.

(f)

Use Desmos and your graph to find the value of \(x\) that gives Pablo and Aisha the box with the biggest volume.

(g)

Algebra Challenge: Use the distributive property to rewrite your volume formula without any parentheses!
