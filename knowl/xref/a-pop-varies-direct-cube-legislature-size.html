Activity 12.2.4.

Political scientists have observed
 4 
R. Taagepera. ``The size of national assemblies.’’ Social Science Research v. 1, no. 4. https://doi.org/10.1016/0049-089X(72)90084-1
that in many countries, the population of the country and the cube of the number of seats in the legislative body most similar to the US House of Representatives are proportional, with constant of proportionality \(c \approx 1\text{.}\) Let \(P\) be the country’s population and \(s\) be the number of seats in the legislative body.
🔗

(a)

Find a power model that describes the relationship between \(P\) and \(s\text{.}\) (Hint: Use the fact that the population is proportional to the cube of the number of seats.)
🔗
🔗

(b)

In 2021, the Canadian House of Commons had 338 seats. Use your model to predict the population of Canada in 2021.
🔗
🔗

(c)

The actual population of Canada in 2021 was about 37 million people. Did your model give a good estimate?
🔗
🔗

(d)

In 2021, the population of México was about 129 million. Use your model to estimate the number of seats in the Cámara de Diputados. Warning You will have to write 129 million as 129,000,000 in order to get the right answer!
🔗
🔗

(e)

The Cámara de Diputados had 500 seats in 2021. Did your model give a good estimate?
🔗
🔗

(f)

The US House of Representatives had 435 voting members in 2021. Use your model to predict the population of the US in 2021.
🔗
🔗

(g)

The population of the US was about 332 million in 2021. Did your model give a good estimate?
🔗
🔗

(h)

Although the model worked well for Canada and México, it didn’t work well for the US! Why? One possible explanation is that the US House of Representatives hasn’t changed size since it was set to 435 by the Apportionment Act of 1911. The US population in 1911 was about 94 million. Would the model have worked better in 1911?
