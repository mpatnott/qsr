Activity 17.2.2.

In Activity 12.2.4, we worked with a power model that relates the population of a country and the size of its lower house (i.e., its legislative body most similar to the US House of Representatives). That model, proposed by Rein Taagepera in 1972
 1 
Taagepera, Rein (1972). “The size of national assemblies.” Social Science Research.1(4): 385-401. doi:10.1016/0049-089X(72)90084-1.
, is often stated as \(s \approx P^{1/3}\text{,}\) that is, the size of the lower house is roughly the cube root of the population.
🔗
Where did the \(\frac13\)rd power come from? And, is it still reasonable? Let’s investigate this question for European legislatures. The table below shows the 2019 populations and lower house size for a sample of European countries

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cube_root_law
