    Worksheet 2.5 Piecewise Linear Models Prep Activities
    A4US

    You will need to be able to interpret the formula for a linear model and build linear models in the next lesson.

    Prep Activity 2.5.1.

    Zeke is driving from his house to the beach and wants to model how far he is from home. He is currently 50 miles from home and will drive 65 mph until he reaches the beach. Let t be time in hours and D be distance in miles from Zeke’s home. Which of the following equations models this situation? Explain your answer in words.
    1. \(\displaystyle D = 50 - 65t\)
    2. \(\displaystyle D = 50 + 65t\)
    3. \(\displaystyle D = 65 - 50t\)
    4. \(\displaystyle D = 65 + 50t\)

    Prep Activity 2.5.2.

    The number of members in a professional organization increased each year for ten years, starting in 2013. The result can be modeled by the equation \(M = 1,200 + 250t\text{.}\)

    (a)

    What do you think the variables \(M\) and \(t\) represent?

    (b)

    What was the rate of the organization’s growth? How do you know?

    (c)

    (i)
    At the start of the growth period?
    (ii)
    At the end of the growth period?
    (iii)
    In 2020?

    Prep Activity 2.5.3.

    Most car values decrease over time. For example, in 2015, a new Toyota Prius Two sold for $25,500. In 2023, online car valuation sites like the Kelley Blue Book and Car Gurus list used 2015 Prius Twos for $18,500. The value of the 2015 Toyota Prius Two depreciated over time.

    (a)

    Assume the depreciation is linear. What is the rate of change of a linear model that represents the value of the 2015 Prius Two over time?

    (b)

    Create a linear model of the value of the 2015 Prius Two, where \(V\) is the value, and \(t\) is the number of years after 2015.

    (c)

    What is the vertical intercept of the graph of the model? What does it represent in the context of this problem?

    (d)

    Use the model to estimate the value of the 2015 Prius Two in 2026. How much confidence do you have in your prediction?
