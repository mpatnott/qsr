    Section 16.2 Inverse Variation and Negative Exponent Activities

    Recall that in Section 16.1, we said that two varying quantities \(Q_1\) and \(Q_2\) have an inversely proportional relationship when the product of the quantities is constant. That is, \(Q_1\) and \(Q_2\) have an inversely proportional relationship when
    \begin{equation*} c = Q_2 Q_1 \end{equation*}
    always has the same value. We call \(c\) the constant of proportionality.
    🔗
    We sometimes reorganize \(c = Q_2 Q_1\) as
    \begin{equation*} Q_2 = \frac{c}{Q_1} \end{equation*}
    and say that \(Q_2\) varies inversely with respect to \(Q_1\text{.}\)
    🔗

    Activity 16.2.1.

    Zipf’s Law says that in a particular text, the number of times a word appears in the text is inversely proportional to its rank (i.e., the most common word has rank 1, the second most common has rank 2, etc.). Let \(N\) be the number of times, in millions, a word appears in English Wikipedia and \(r\) be its rank.
    🔗
    According to one source
     1 
    https://github.com/IlyaSemenov/wikipedia-word-frequency
    , “the” was the most common word in English Wikipedia in 2023, appearing 187 times.
    🔗

    (a)

    What are the values of \(r\) and \(N\) for “the” in English Wikipedia?
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Use your values of \(r\) and \(N\) to find the constant of proportionality.
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Set up a formula that outputs \(N\) when given \(r\text{.}\) That is, write a formula that relates \(r\) and \(N\) where \(N\) is by itself on one side of the equals sign, and the other side of the equations sign is an expression that includes \(r\text{.}\)
    🔗

    (d)

    Use your formula to estimate the number of times each of the following words appears in English Wikipedia:
    🔗

    “of”, the second most common word

    “is”, the eight most common word

    Activity 16.2.6.

    A radar system sends out radio waves, which bounce off objects in the world and return to the system. The radar system interprets the signals that it receives back to learn information such as how far away the object. The signal gets weaker the entire time it is traveling. In order for a radar system to detect an object, it has to be close enough that the signal’s strength when it arrives back is detectable by the system. Physics tells us that the signal power the system will receive when the signal returns is inversely proportional to the fourth power of the distance the signal has to travel to the object
     2 
    https://www.radartutorial.eu/01.basics/The%20Radar%20Range%20Equation.en.html
    .

    The relationship between signal power and distance is a power model. What is its exponent?
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Suppose a particular radar system receives a return signal of 4231 picowatts (pW) from a known target 20 kilometers (km) away. Use your equation to find the constant of proportionality. Your answer should be a big number! Write it in scientific notation, and round so that there are 2 decimal places in scientific notation.
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    What signal power should this system receive back from a target that is 25 km away?
